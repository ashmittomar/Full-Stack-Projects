Nature’s ID Card

Every species of plant and animal carries its own official “identity” through scientific and common names. Just like Hibiscus rosa-sinensis (China rose) or Elephas maximus (Asian elephant), these names prevent confusion and ensure correct identification for research and conservation.

The Global Language of Species

While common names vary—like Indian peafowl, “mor,” or “mayil”—the scientific name Pavo cristatus speaks universally. This global recognition helps scientists, farmers, and conservationists coordinate across borders without miscommunication.

Stories Behind the Names

Many names carry centuries of meaning. The lotus (Nelumbo nucifera) stands for purity in many Asian cultures, while the Bengal tiger (Panthera tigris tigris) is a symbol of strength and biodiversity. Some names even reveal ecological roles—like “honeybee” hinting at pollination and honey production.
